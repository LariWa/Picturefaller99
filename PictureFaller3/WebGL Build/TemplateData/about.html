The project „When Science Connects with Games“ has been developed as a part of the curriculum of University for Applied Science Berlin (HTW Berlin).
The project has started as a possible answer to the problem of manual testing of image sorting algorithms – it is very difficult to make real humans choose the same images over and over again.
It is also, of course, not possible to measure how good the algorithms are with brute force.
In order to have a good understanding of what works for human eye, one also needs to test the algorithms with real people.
The game`s premise is a dream world, where in order to continue dreaming, the player needs to solve little riddles – this is where the testing comes into play.
The project has been designed to make the boring task engaging and, possibly, positively addictive for the players. The project has been presented on a Showtime fair and the person that has managed to solve the most puzzles, has won a prize.
The game has been created by seven students of International Media and Computing: Andreas Wiedl, Jodie Preuß, Larissa Wagnerberger, Mert Sevindik, Nikolas Joop, Tayeeb Hussain and Veronika Bondarenko.
The team of seven has delegated different tasks to smaller teams (2-3 people) in order to work fast and efficiently. The development process has lasted for a semester (4 months) and has been supervised by Professor Kai-Uwe Barthel and Konstantin Schall.
